Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

Prolific and influential composer of the Classical Period

Here's a time line of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart's life:

  • 1756 - Birth of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
  • 1761 - First Public Performance
  • 1762 - Mozart and his sister travel to Munich and Vienna, performing for the court.
  • 1763 - Young Wolfgang begins to compose his first pieces.
  • 1768 - Mozart writes his first opera, "La Finte Simplice".
  • 1777 - For several years as a young man, Mozart travelled throughout Europe composing
  • 1782 - Mozart had settled in Vienna, producing a number of operas and other compositions during this period.
  • 1786 - Mozart and his wife twice travelled to Prague for the premieres of two of Mozart's operas, first "The Marriage of Figaro" and later "Don Giovanni."
  • 1787 - Beethoven arrived in Vienna to study with Mozart.
  • 1788 - Mozart's compositions began to sell more poorly around 1788. He continued to play, but concerts were less well attended.
  • 1789 - Between 1789 and 1790, Mozart composed "Cosi fan Tutte," and may have composed a piece for the coronation of Joseph II.
  • 1791 - ozart found renewed success, first with "The Magic Flute." He began work on the Requiem, but did not complete it.
  • 1791 - On December 5, 1791, while still in his thirties, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart died not long after the composition and success of "The Magic Flute." His cause of death is recorded as fever.
